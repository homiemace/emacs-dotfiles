#+title: MAC address
#+filetags: :cybersecurity:
* Big Questions

** What is this?

*** A MAC address is a hardware-related identifier used by a network interface on a local network.

*** It is used on the local network to help deliver data to the correct device.

*** MAC addresses work at Layer 2 of networking, unlike IP addresses, which work at Layer 3.

** Why is this important?

*** MAC addresses are necessary for communication on a local network.

*** Devices use them to send data to the correct network interface nearby.

*** They are important in networking and cybersecurity because they appear in things like ARP, switch forwarding, MAC spoofing, and device identification.

** When will I need this?

*** When learning how devices communicate on a [[id:033e85ba-78b1-450d-8eaa-62658fda889e][LAN]]

*** When troubleshooting local network issues

*** When studying ARP, switches, and packet/frame delivery

*** When learning about MAC spoofing, ARP poisoning, or network monitoring in cybersecurity

** How does it work?

*** A device sends data on the local network using a MAC address.

*** If it knows the other device’s IP address but not its MAC, it uses ARP to find it.

*** If the data is going somewhere outside the network, it sends it to the router’s MAC address first.

** Quick clarification
*** Local network = same network
*** Across networks = different networks
*** MAC = local delivery
*** IP = across-network delivery
